Glacier National Park

About Glacier

Glacier National Park is Located in northwestern Montana, along the Canadian Border. The park contains over one million acres and encompasses two mountain ranges: subranges of the Rocky Mountains. Glacier also hold over 1,000 species of plants, hundreds of animal species, and more than 130 lakes! It has been coined the "Crown of the Continent Ecosystem". The park still has most of the original native animal and plant species. There are large mammals like Grizzly bears , mountain goats , moose , and endangered species such as Canadian Lynxes and wolverines.

Listen to a grizzly bear roar!



Glacier has multiple ecosystems from tundra to prairie, where some ecosystems are privy to forest fires. There has been a fire every year of the parks existence, except for 1964. The most fires on record occurred in 1936, with 64 fires. The park borders Waterton Lakes National Park , which is a park in Canada (Glacier National Park, 2023).

Glaciers and Climate Change.

The mountains of Glacier National Park were carved by glaciers of the last ice age. The vast majority of these glaciers have since melted over the last 12,000 years, and remaining glaciers continue to melt due to rising climate temperatures. One of the most prominent glaciers is Grinnell Glacier which was named after George Bird Grinnell, and early American Conservationist, who was an advocate for Glacier National Park's creation. Grinnell is the most photographed glacier in the park, located in the Lewis Range at Many Glacier, at an altitude of around 7,000 feet. Between 1966 and 2005, Grinnell has lost around 40% of its size and glaciologists predict that projections continue at this rate, all the glaciers in the park will disappear by 2030. (Glacier National Park, 2023).
