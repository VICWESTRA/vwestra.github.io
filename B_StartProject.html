Acadia National Park


About the Park

Acadia National Park is located in Coastal Maine, primarily on Mount Desert Island, with other portions on smaller islands. The park was established in 1916 and was named after the French settlers who were expelled from Atlantic Canada By the British. The park contains 49,052 acres of land and receives over 3.5 million visitors each year! (Yogerst, 2019). Acadia contains the tallest mountain on the Atlantic Coast of the United States, better known as Cadillac Mountain. Cadillac Mountain is also one of the first places in the United States to see the sun rise! ("Acadia National Park").

Geography

There are 26 significant mountains which rise in Acadia, offering expansive views of lakes, forests, islands, and the ocean. The mountains range from 284 feet tall (Flying Mountain) to 1,530 feet tall (Cadillac Mountain). The main attraction in Acadia is Park Loop road, which is a 27-mile road that leads to many scenic viewpoints, trailheads, and to the top of Cadillac Mountain. Some prominent features along the road are Champlain Mountain, the Beehive, Sand Beach, Thunder Hole, Gorham Mountain, Otter Cliff, Otter Cove, Jordan Pond, and Eagle Lake ("Acadia National Park").

The Fire of 1947

In 1947, the most influential forest fire in Acadia history occurred, where over 10,000 acres of the park burned and an additional 7,000 acres of Mount Desert Island burned. The fire began on October 17, 1947 and continued burning until November 14th, 1947. The following restoration of the park was financially supported by the Rockefeller family. ("Acadia National Park").
